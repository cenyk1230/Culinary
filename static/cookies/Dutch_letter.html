Dutch letter

The Dutch letter (also referred to as banket letter,

The pastry was originally shaped "into the initial of the family's surname." and during some festivals in the United States.

Etymology

The pastry's name is a shortened version of the Dutch word banketletter.

History

In the Netherlands letterbanket are traditionally eaten on Sinterklaasavond on December 5, where they are shaped in the initials of family members.

Dutch letters were introduced in the United States by Dutch immigrants,

Dutch letters prepared with chocolate are traditionally eaten as part of the activities during the Holland Dutch Winterfest in Holland, Michigan.
