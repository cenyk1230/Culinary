Jammie Dodgers

Jammie Dodgers are a popular British biscuit, made from shortbread with a raspberry flavoured jam filling. They are currently produced by Burton's Biscuit Company at its factory in Llantarnam. In 2009, Jammie Dodgers were the most popular children's sweet biscuit brand in the United Kingdom, with 40% of the years' sales consumed by adults.

Named after Roger the Dodger from The Beano comics, In 2011, the brand was re-launched under the "Dodgers" umbrella with two new products: Toffee and Choccie.

A version of this biscuit is made in France under the name "Gateaux Sables Nappage Fraise" by Pat'Boul de Provence. This version is bigger (100mm (3.9in) diameter) and has three round holes through which strawberry jam is visible.
