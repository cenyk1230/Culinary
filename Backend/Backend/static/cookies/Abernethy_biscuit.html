Abernethy biscuit

The Abernethy biscuit was invented by doctor John Abernethy in the 18th century as a digestive improver and hence aid to health.

Abernethy believed that most diseases were due to disorders in digestion. The Abernethy biscuit is a type of digestive biscuit, a baked good originally designed to be eaten as a support to proper digestion.

Abernethy biscuits remain popular in Scotland. They are manufactured commercially by Simmers (Edinburgh), Walls Bakeries (Shetland Islands), and by Stag Bakeries (Isle of Lewis).
