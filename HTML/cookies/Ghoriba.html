A ghoriba (also spelled ghribia, ghraïba, or ghriyyaba) is a type of biscuit prepared in the Maghreb and parts of the Middle East. It is a round, shortbread cookie made with flour, sugar, butter, and usually almonds. It is often served with coffee or Maghrebi mint tea. They are similar to polvorón from Andalusia and qurabiya from Iran.[1][2][3]

Regional variations[edit]

Algeria[edit]

Ghribia (Arabic: غريبية‎) [4][5][6]

  • Ghribia with almonds
  • Ghribia with peanuts
  • Ghribia with walnuts
  • Ghribia with pistachios

Morocco[edit]

Ghoriba (Arabic: غْرِيبَة‎)

  • Mlouwza, made with almonds and sugar flavored with orange flower water
  • Ghoriba bahla
  • Ghoriba dyal zite
  • Ghoriba mramla

Tunisia[edit]

Ghraïba (Arabic: غريبة‎)

  • Ghraïba bidha, made with wheat flour
  • Ghraïba droô, made with sorghum flour
  • Ghraïba homs, made with chickpea flour
